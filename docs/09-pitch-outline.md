## Slide 1: The Hook

**Visual:** Side-by-side — WhatsApp group labeled "Convention Electricians 2026" vs. TrustGrid dashboard

**Headline:** "Every community in Nigeria manages its workforce on WhatsApp."

**Subtext:** This is not just inefficient. It is dangerous.

**Speaking notes:**

> "At a major national convention last year, an unverified worker caused an electrical incident. Nobody knew he had a prior record — because that record was in someone else's phone."

> "This is the way communities, estates, schools, and churches manage their service workers today. And it creates a trust gap that costs institutions millions and puts communities at risk."

## Slide 2: The Problem

**Visual:** Four pain points with icons

| Pain | Cost |
|------|------|
| No worker verification | ₦100K–₦5M per incident |
| No performance history | Reactive hiring, repeated mistakes |
| No institutional memory | Lost when staff changes |
| No workforce scale | Manual chaos for 200-person events |

**Headline:** "The problem is not finding workers. It's trusting them."

**Speaking notes:**

> "The common assumption is that communities need a marketplace — a place to discover workers. That is wrong."

> "Every estate manager already knows 20 electricians. Every church already has a contact for every service category. The problem is governance: who can we trust, who performed well, who has incidents, who should we never hire again?"

## Slide 4: The Solution

**Visual:** Architecture diagram (simplified) + 5 core capabilities

**Headline:** "TrustGrid: The Trust Operating System for Communities"

**Five capabilities:**
1. **Verify** — Multi-adapter identity verification (NIN, BVN, Ghana Card)
2. **Track** — Permanent performance and deployment history
3. **Score** — Algorithmic, configurable trust scores
4. **Govern** — Workforce assignment, procurement, incident management
5. **Scale** — From 50 workers to 50,000

**Key message:**

> "Not a marketplace. Not a directory. The governance infrastructure that institutions use to manage trusted people at scale."

## Presentation Tips

**Do:**
- Speak from conviction — you understand this problem deeply
- Use specific numbers (not "a lot" — say "₦5M per incident")
- Reference Redemption City specifically — it's being built in front of the judges
- Let the demo do the talking — don't over-explain what the judges can see

**Don't:**
- Say "artisan marketplace" — that is not TrustGrid
- Say "like Uber for handymen" — this undersells the platform
- Apologize for limitations — MVP is intentional, not incomplete
- Over-explain the tech — they care about impact, not architecture

**Killer lines (use them):**
- "Trust is infrastructure, not a feature."
- "We are not building a marketplace. We are building the operating system."
- "Every worker who joins TrustGrid makes every institution on TrustGrid better."
- "When Redemption City has one million people, what infrastructure will they use to govern trusted services? That is TrustGrid."
