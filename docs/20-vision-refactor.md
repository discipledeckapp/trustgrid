# TrustGrid — Vision Refactor v2.0

**Status:** Canonical. Supersedes all previous product positioning documents.
**Date:** June 2026

---

## What Changed and Why

Earlier versions of TrustGrid positioned the product primarily as:
- Workforce governance software
- Procurement management
- Service marketplace infrastructure

This framing was too narrow. It described features, not the underlying idea.

The correct framing is:

> **TrustGrid is Community Trust Infrastructure.**

---

## The Core Insight

Every organized community — a church, an estate, a university, a professional association — has the same unsolved problem.

**They cannot govern trust at scale.**

They cannot answer:
- Who actually belongs here?
- Who has been verified?
- Who has authority to speak for this community?
- Who endorsed this person, and do they have the standing to do so?
- What is this person's reputation across our community?
- Who should see this opportunity?
- Can this person access this facility?

These questions exist at every level of every organized community in the world.

And today, the answers live in WhatsApp messages, personal phone books, memory, and informal verbal hierarchies.

---

## The Primary Asset Is Not Software

The most defensible asset TrustGrid builds is not the application.

It is the **Trust Graph**.

The Trust Graph is the accumulated network of:
- Verified identities
- Community memberships
- Authority relationships
- Endorsements (with their authority chain)
- Credentials
- Reputation history
- Assignment history
- Community relationships

Every deployment, every endorsement, every verified membership makes the Trust Graph more valuable.

**The Trust Graph is permanent institutional memory at community scale.**

## What TrustGrid Is

TrustGrid provides the infrastructure communities need to answer the question:

**"Who can be trusted here, and why?"**

Through:
- **Community Hierarchy Engine** — model any organizational structure
- **Authority Engine** — assign, delegate, and verify authority
- **Membership Management** — verified community belonging
- **Endorsement Engine** — authority-backed trust signals
- **Trust Passport** — portable, verifiable reputation identity
- **Trust Credential Engine** — digital credentials with chain-of-authority
- **Opportunity Network** — trust-gated access to roles and opportunities
- **Real-Time Verification** — QR-based credential verification for any context

## RCCG Convention Demo (Restructured)

**Old narrative:** "Operations team needs 50 electricians"

**New narrative:** "Area 5 needs to staff the annual Holy Ghost Congress"

1. Operations team opens the **Opportunity Network** — publishes "200 volunteers needed, Holy Ghost Congress, Lagos Zone"
2. Opportunity is tagged: requires *Active Parish Membership* + *Trust Score ≥ 65*
3. 1,247 eligible members across 14 parishes see the opportunity
4. Members apply — their Trust Passports are reviewed
5. Area Coordinator (Authority Role) approves deployment
6. Deployed members get a temporary **Convention Credential** on their Trust Passport
7. Security scans QR at gate — sees: Active Member, Convention Credential, Authorized Area

This story is bigger than "staffing an event." It is **community governance at scale**.

TrustGrid connects:
- Residents → Services → Trust

**TrustGrid is the operating system for the city's trust layer.**
